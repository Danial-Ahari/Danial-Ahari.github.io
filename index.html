Archival Work

One of the many positions I've held at my university has been working in the Special Collections and Archives. This work may not seem to overlap with my field of study, but in truth, there is a drought of computer literate archival employees, and as such, much of the digital collections available on the web are being done by volunteers or are maintained in such a way that makes them difficult to access. I work in the Archives, mainly out of my interest, but have conducted a cross-disciplinary research and creative project to help with the digital representation of knowledge.

Furthermore, archival storage typically resides on media that is read by computers, because they are the most convenient, and most long-term trustworthy method for storing data in the 21st century. Particularly for older digital formats that are becoming harder to access, this data is sometimes recovered and stored in ways that are less than optimal, because they do not store bit-for-bit representations or because they do not store all data, even that which is hidden. This makes the fields of Computer Science and Computer Engineering applicable to archival science, in that we must design practical, easy-to-use, efficient-to-store ways of retaining older digital data and converted analogue data in a lossless way.
